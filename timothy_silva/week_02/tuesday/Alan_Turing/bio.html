B I O G R A P H Y
  • Alan Turing, born London 23 June 1912 - 7 June 1954
  • Considered a father of modern day computing
  • A computer scientist, mathematician, logician, cryptanalyst and theoretical biologist
  • When WW2 broke out he took a leading role in deciphering the German ciphers out of Bletchley Park.
  • It is postulated that due to his role in helping crack the enigma codes the war was shortened by 2-4 years and 14 to 28 millions lives were saved
  • He was found dead by his cleaner on 8 June 1954. He had died the day before of cyanide poisoning, a half-eaten apple beside his bed.
  • It is still a mystery whether he committed suicide, was killed or accidentally poisoned himself whilst using cyanide in amateur chemistry experiments.
